1. Introduction and Scope of Services: Briefly describe the company and the types of computer support services offered. Define what is considered "computer support" within the context of the agreement, including potential service levels or packages.

2. Client Responsibilities: Outline what the client is expected to do, such as providing access to their computers or devices, ensuring data backups, and promptly responding to communication from the support team.

3. Company Responsibilities: Specify what the company will do, including troubleshooting, repairs, software installations, and training. Outline the company's commitment to data protection and confidentiality.

4. Payment Terms: Define the payment schedule, including due dates, payment methods, and any applicable fees. Clearly outline any late payment penalties or service interruption due to non-payment.

5. Intellectual Property: State that the company retains ownership of its software and tools, and the client cannot copy, distribute, or modify them without permission.

6. Liability and Disclaimer: Limit the company's liability for any damage to the client's equipment or data, including disclaimers for unforeseen issues like virus infections or hard drive failures. Specify that the company is not responsible for data loss due to hardware failures or natural disasters.

7. Privacy and Security: Include a link to the company's privacy policy, outlining how client data will be handled and protected.

8. Termination of Service: Explain how either party can terminate the agreement, including any notice periods and refund policies.

9. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution: Specify the governing law and how disputes will be resolved, such as through arbitration or legal action.

10. Contact Information: Provide the company's contact information for inquiries and support requests.
